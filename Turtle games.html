Project Context

The workflow began with comprehensive data preparation, including cleaning, ingestion, and wrangling of the datasets in both python and R environments. The subsequent analysis was divided into two main areas of focus.

The first area concentrated on customer-related data, aiming to extract insights regarding customer behaviour and trends.

The second area focused on product data, evaluating various product-related metrics. This dual approach enabled a holistic understanding of both customer and product dimensions within the organisation.

Analytical Approach

Upon exploring the data sets, it is discovered that a lot of reviews are left by the same customer for different products. For the investigation of loyalty points accumulation and high potential customers using regression analysis, the duplicates with same values of gender, age, remuneration, spending score, loyalty points, education were dropped because duplicates affects the accuracy of predictive models. On the other hand, the data set with the duplicates was used for sentiment analysis which focuses on the products, review and summary.

The distribution of the loyalty points is highly skewed to the right. Since the loyalty points is the dependent variable in the regression analysis, transformation of the data was carried out to improve the regression analysis. The details of the transformation can be found in the Jupyter notebook and r scripts.

Clustering using k-means deemed to be suitable because there are dense regions where the customers grouped around specific spending score and remuneration. Elbow and silhouette analysis were carried out and several models were build and compared. Group of high potential customers were identified based on the result of the clustering.

NLP analysis using Textblob was carried out and revealed that the opinion of customers towards the products are generally positive. However, there are some misleading score of the polarity. Combination of the sentiment score with the product was carried out to identify the best and the worst products for future action.

Key Findings

  • There is a positive association between loyalty points, remuneration and spending scores.
  • A MLR model is built to forecast the loyalty points but the accuracy of the model is undermined by the presence of heteroscedasticity.
  • Customers is grouped to 5 clusters and Cluster 0 and Cluster 3 are labelled as high potential customers for target market.
  • Sentiment analysis (NLP analysis) confirmed that customers have positive opinion on the products in general.

Recommendations

  • The MLR model should be utilised with cautions. Removal of outliers is recommended to tune the model for better accuracy.
  • While the loyalty points tends to be higher for customers with higher remuneration and spending score, strategies to engage lower-income with high loyalty points (Cluster 0) and strategies to engage higher-income with high loyalty points (Cluster 3) should be explored.
  • Review the comments on the top 10 most favourite products and explore their potential for future market.
  • Review the comments on the top 10 least favourite products to address any issues related to the products.
  • Collect data with timeline to make time series prediction and discover time-based trend in customers’ opinion towards the products.
