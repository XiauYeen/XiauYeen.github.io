Exploratory Analysis using Excel and SQL:
A project for 2Market

Project Context

2Market is a global supermarket that sells products online and in-store. They aim to understand their customers' demographics and purchasing behaviour. Specifically, 2Market wants to learn about:

  • The demographics of their customers.
  • Which advertising channels are the most effective.
  • Which products sell best and how sales vary by demographic.

  • Presentation deck

Analytical Approach

The data is provided in two CSV files. Since the data size is small, initial data analysis was performed using Excel. The data was cleaned before exporting it to PostgreSQL.

SQL queries were used to pivot the product columns and advertising channel columns from the raw data into rows. Several tables and views were created and exported into Tableau for visualisation.

Dashboard Design

The dashboard was designed to provide insights into the demographics of customers, the effectiveness of advertising channels, and the sales performance of products. Inspired by the dashboards in Tableau Public, I have chosen to use:

  • Text colour that meets minimum contrast guidelines.
  • Clean visuals by removing unnecessary labels and gridlines.
  • Simplified text to remove redundancy.
  • Various charts to keep interest.
  • Different colours for different segments.
  • Accessible filter.

Key Findings

  • 76% of the customers aged between 30 and 60.
  • Most of the customers are from Spain, account for 49% of global sales.
  • Customers aged between 40 to 50 years old with income between $60k and $90k have the highest purchasing power.
  • Liquor is the product that generates the highest sales across all countries.
  • Twitter, bulkmail and Instagram are the top 3 platforms with high lead conversions in general.
    However, the effectiveness of the advertising channels varies by country.

Recommendations

  • Customers aged 40 to 50 would be the primary target customers of 2Market.
  • Marketing strategies should be vary by country to account for local environments.
  • The margin of the product sales should be analysed to identify the product that generates the highest profit.
  • Future analysis should examine the customer purchasing preferences and satisfaction levels.
